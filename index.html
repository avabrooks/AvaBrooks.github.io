About

I'm a data analyst and quantitative researcher with a background in Mathematics and International Political Economy from Fordham University. I recently graduated and am beginning my career at Brooklyn Sports & Entertainment, supporting the Barclays Center, Brooklyn Nets, and NY Liberty across IT, consumer technology, and data analytics.

My experience spans election data engineering at the Associated Press, sports analytics at Perfect Game and Sportradar, digital policy research at GCOCT, and grant data analysis at the Marcus Harris Foundation. I move comfortably between technical work and strategic communication.

I care about rigor, reproducibility, and making complex findings accessible to non-technical audiences. Dean's List recipient and AP Scholar with Distinction.

03

Projects

001

Does Digital Campaigning Mobilize Young Voters?

↗

Feb — Apr 2026  ·  Country First Academy  ·  Capstone Research

Investigated whether Meta and Google digital ad spending predicts youth voter turnout in the 2024 presidential election. Built a reproducible state-level dataset and ran multiple OLS regression models across all 50 states.

  • Data Engineering — Parsed $7.46B in political ad spend from the Meta Ad Library and Google Transparency Center via custom Python scripts
  • Regression Modeling — Eight OLS specifications testing digital spend, demographic controls, and FEC disbursements against state-level youth turnout
  • County-Level Analysis — Extended the model to 511 counties across seven battleground states using Census API data and MIT Election Lab returns
  • Messaging Strategy — Developed 12 platform-native voter mobilization concepts segmented by party, gender, and platform
Python OLS Regression Census API Meta Ad Library Political Research
002

Comparing Tax Policies in the 2024 U.S. Presidential Election

↗

Aug — Dec 2024  ·  Fordham University  ·  Academic Research

Comprehensive analysis of 2024 tax policy proposals from Trump and Harris, examining implications for income distribution, government revenue, and economic growth.

  • Quantitative Analysis — Evaluated projected tax burdens across income classes using structured data
  • Federal & California Case Studies — Assessed regional impacts based on economic conditions
  • Mathematical Modeling — Developed differential equations to model long-term effects of tax policy on revenue and growth
  • Visualizations — Illustrated key fiscal trends using TikZ; produced a structured report in LaTeX with Beamer presentation
